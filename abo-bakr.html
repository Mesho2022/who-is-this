Muhammad ibn Zakariya al-Razi (250 AH/864 AD - 5 Shaban 311 AH / November 19, 923 AD)

Abu Bakr Muhammad ibn Yahya ibn Zakaria al-Razi was a Muslim physician, chemist, philosopher and mathematician who was one of the scholars of the golden age of science. », where he wrote the book Al-Hawi on Medicine, which included all medical knowledge from the days of the Greeks until 925 AD and remained the main medical reference in Europe for 400 years after that date, studying mathematics, medicine, philosophy, astronomy, chemistry, logic and literature. In Al-Ray, Al-Razi became famous and traveled the country and worked as head of a hospital. He had many letters in various fields of diseases and wrote in all branches of medicine and knowledge at that time, some of which were translated into Latin so that the main references in medicine continued until the seventeenth century. Among his greatest books are “History of Medicine” and a book Al-Mansour in Medicine and the book “Al-Dawaa al-Mufradah” which includes an accurate description of the anatomy of the body’s organs. He was the first to invent surgical threads, make ointments, and has books on pharmacology that contributed to the advancement of pharmacology. He has 200 books and articles on various aspects of science.

His life and upbringing

There are different and conflicting opinions about the life of Abu Bakr Muhammad ibn Yahya ibn Zakaria al-Razi, who is believed to have been born in the city of Rayy, near modern Tehran. Most likely, he was born in the year 251 AH / 865 AD. Al-Razi was of the opinion that students should learn the medical industry in the large crowded cities, where there were many patients and skilled doctors practiced their profession. Therefore, Rayan spent his youth in the city of peace, and studied medicine in Baghdad. Historians have mistakenly assumed that Al-Razi learned medicine after he was old. And I came to know this fact from a text in a manuscript in the Bodley Library in Oxford, entitled “Experiences” from what Muhammad ibn Baghdad wrote in his youth, and this text was published accompanied by excerpts on the same subject, which I quoted from the books of Al-Razi that he wrote after his experience was completed, and in which his style bears witness his own opinion. After completing his medical studies in Baghdad, Al-Razi returned to the city of Rayy at the invitation of its ruler, Mansur bin Ishaq, to ​​take over the management of Al Ray Hospital. Al-Razi wrote for this ruler his book “Al-Mansoori fi Al-Tibb” and then “Al-Tibb Al-Ruhani” and both complement the other. The first is concerned with diseases of the body, and the second with diseases of the soul. Then he moved again to Baghdad to take over the presidency of the new Al-Mutadidi, which was established by the Caliph Al-Mutadidi Billah (279-289 AH/892-902AD).
